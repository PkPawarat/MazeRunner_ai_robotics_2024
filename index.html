Welcome to Maze Runner

MazeRunner is a project aimed at implementing Deep Reinforcement Learning (DRL) for autonomous maze navigation. The objective is to develop an AI-powered robot capable of navigating through a maze to reach a goal in a specific order. This project is part of the "Artificial Intelligence (AI) in Robotics" subject at the University of Technology Sydney. The primary objective is to develop an AI capable of executing a specific task using machine learning techniques. We focused on creating an autonomous vehicle designed to traverse a maze by learning to follow a path using Deep Q-Network (DQN) reinforcement learning while avoiding obstacles. The goal is to simulate a real-world scenario where an autonomous vehicle navigates through hazardous or dangerous areas, making real-time decisions to follow the path and avoid obstacles to reach its destination safely.

Project Details

Our project was an iterative process of continuous improvement and collaboration. Here's a brief look at the main steps we undertook:

  • Initial Ideation: Brainstorming sessions to identify the project's scope and objectives.
  • Planning: Formulating a detailed project plan with clearly defined milestones.
  • Development: Writing and refining code to develop the autonomous vehicle model.
  • Testing and Evaluation: Rigorously testing the model and fine-tuning it based on the results.
  • Documentation: Comprehensive documentation of all stages of the project.
  • Final Presentation: Preparing a polished presentation to demonstrate our project.

Results

The results of our project demonstrated the effectiveness of using DQN for autonomous navigation. Our vehicle successfully learned to navigate the maze with minimal errors. Key outcomes include:

  • Effective Path Following: The vehicle learned to follow the path efficiently.
  • Obstacle Avoidance: The vehicle was able to avoid obstacles effectively.
  • Performance Metrics: Detailed analysis and metrics to showcase the performance of the model.

Our reward system played a crucial role in training the autonomous vehicle. The key components of the reward system are:

  • Base Reward: A time penalty applied to encourage the vehicle to find the optimal path quickly.
  • Distance Reward: Rewards for reducing the distance to the goal.
  • Smooth Driving Reward: Rewards for maintaining a smooth driving path.
  • Obstacle Avoidance Reward: Additional rewards for avoiding obstacles.
  • Collision Penalty: Penalties for colliding with walls or obstacles.
  • Goal Reward: Significant rewards for reaching a goal, with extra rewards for reaching the final goal.
